<p><p>Carcinoid syndrome management encompasses various treatment approaches for controlling symptoms and tumor growth. Chemotherapy utilizes cytotoxic drugs to target rapidly dividing cells, aiming to reduce tumor size and improve symptoms. Biological therapy focuses on agents that leverage the immune system or block specific pathways to inhibit tumor progression. Hepatic artery embolization (HAE) involves delivering agents directly into blood vessels feeding the tumors, effectively reducing blood flow and limiting tumor growth, particularly in liver metastases. Each approach addresses different aspects of the syndrome.</p></p>
