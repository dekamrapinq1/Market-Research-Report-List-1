<p><p>Thru beam sensors are widely used in automation systems for object detection and counting. The market can be divided into two primary types based on output configurations: NPN and PNP sensors. NPN sensors produce a low output signal when triggered, typically sinking current to ground, making them suitable for common negative setups. Conversely, PNP sensors supply a high output signal, sourcing current from the power supply, ideal for common positive configurations. Both types play crucial roles in various industrial applications, enhancing operational efficiency.</p></p>
